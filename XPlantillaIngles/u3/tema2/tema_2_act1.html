Learning activity 1

Parents of teenagers often complain about their children not tiding things around the house. Even adults find themselves being a little disorganized. What we have not done yet and what we have already done keep our mind busy all day long.

Open the file Family conflicts from Oxeden, Clive, & Latham-Koenig Christina; as a self-study task. Do exercise 1a and b. Then go to the Grammar section in the same file. Read the conversations, match them with the pictures and find just, yet and already examples in the conversations. What tense is the verb with them? What do you think they mean?.

Now, study the information in the Present Perfect + yet, just, already. It will help you understand this tense as well as their meaning and use.

To complete the activity answer exercise 4a and 4b in Grammar Practice file. Send your sentences in online text.

The grammar reference and the texts are good examples on how to use Present Perfect along with yet, just and already.

This learning activity will help you reinforce the use Present Perfect as you describe past events and facts that continue in the present.
