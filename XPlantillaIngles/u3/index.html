Introduction
Introduction

Most of what we talk and write comes from our everyday experiences. Conversations topics are about money, family, fears, success and changes issues we deal with as part of our life. Knowing how to speak, read and write about that in English is part of learning the language and being able to communicate in it.

This unit aims at providing you with language exposure and language practice that will help you to communicate ideas and events that began in the past and continue in the present. The materials included in this unit will all make a reference to Present Perfect and Present Perfect Continuous tenses in English.

The activities you will be required to carry out will also be diverse. They go from simple self-study exercises to audio recording of your own information. Special attention will also be drawn to grammatical tenses and forms: Present Perfect vs. Simple Past tense. In addition, vocabulary will be dealt with as you move though the reading texts and audio materials.

The purpose of the whole unit is to provide students with enough opportunities to activate previous knowledge of English, to revise and learn grammar aspects of the language that are used to refer to present and past. Grammar resources have been designed in such a way as to provide students with a clear presentation, guided and free practice, while production tasks need the mastering of key structures. Students will develop listening skills through realistic tasks which reinforce the mastering of the language.

Didactic unit objective(s)
Didactic unit objective(s)

In this didactic unit, the student will recognize Present Perfect form and use; distinguish the use of Present Perfect from Simple Past and Simple Present tenses; communicate ideas about his/her personal life; acquire vocabulary related to money issues.

Learning result(s)
Learning result(s)

You are competent when:

  • You area able to read short simple texts and look for specific information.
  • You are able to understand phrases and common vocabulary on common areas, such as money issues.
  • You are able to communicate in oral and written form personal matters in present and past.
  • You are able to communicate with accurately by using the appropriate tense.
  • You can talk about every day issues.
  • You are able to read and write using the present perfect as well as the past simple in an appropriate way.
  • You can listen to specific information
  • You are able to understand and use in an appropriate way the present perfect continuous tense in order to express unfinished actions.

Glossary
Glossary

Give up
– If you give up a habit [e.g. smoking, drinking] or give up something unhealthy [e.g. cigarettes, alcohol], you stop doing it or having it
– To stop doing a regular activity or job

Look after
– To take care of someone or something by doing what is needed to keep them well or in good condition
– To be responsible for dealing with something

Set up
– To start a company or organization
– To make arrangements so that something can happen or exist
– To give someone the money that they need to start a business
– To start working for yourself in your own company

Take out
– To remove something from a particular place or container
– To borrow a book from a library
– To get money from a bank

Throw away
– To get rid of something because you do not want or need it any more
– To waste a skill or an opportunity to do something good

Turn up
– If someone turns up somewhere, they arrive at that place
– If someone or something turns up, they appear or are found, either after a long time or when you are not expecting them

Reference:
Cambridge University Press (1997) Cambridge International Dictionary of Phrasal Verbs. United Kingdom.

Summary of the didactic unit
Summary of the didactic unit

Students will work on topics from their everyday life. The vocabulary in this unit is presented in a meaningful context, using interesting reading texts, and it is practiced and extended throughout the unit. The whole unit aims at providing the students with opportunities to practice the language and expand knowledge. The grammar structure is presented in a way that it is easy to understand and guides students to successfully achieve the objectives of the unit. There are also reading texts which stories and conversation, all related to the main grammar focus of the unit. Students are invited to explore and get familiar with the new vocabulary through a variety of activities.
