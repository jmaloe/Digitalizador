Introduction
Introduction

The topic of this unit is about cities and lifestyle. It aims at providing you with language exposure and language practice that will help you to make comparisons about cities and places, in addition to express most outstanding qualities from a place. The materials included in this unit will make reference to Comparatives and Superlatives. It will also address quantifiers and phrasal verbs in English.

The activities you will be required to carry out will also be diverse. They go from simple self-study exercises to audio recording of your own information. Special attention will also be drawn to grammatical tenses and forms: Present Perfect vs. Simple Past tense. Besides, vocabulary will be dealt with as you move though the reading texts and audio materials.

The purpose of the whole unit is to provide students with enough opportunities to activate previous knowledge of English, to revise and learn grammar aspects of the language that are used to refer to present and past. Grammar resources have been designed in such a way as to provide students with a clear presentation, guided and free practice, while production tasks need the mastering of key structures. Students will develop listening skills through realistic tasks which reinforce the mastering of the language.

Didactic unit objective(s)
Didactic unit objective(s)

In this didactic unit, the student will recognize and use comparatives and superlatives; express ideas with quantifiers and phrasal verbs; as well as communicate ideas about personal lifestyle.

Learning result(s)
Learning result(s)

You are competent when:

  • You can read for general comprehension.
  • You recognize grammatical patterns.
  • You use acceptable grammatical systems.
  • You can communicate about everyday issues.

Glossary
Glossary

Fall over (falls, falling, fell, fallen).
 1. If someone falls over, they fall to the ground.
2. If something falls over, it falls onto its side.

Find out.
To get information about something because you want to know more about it, or to learn a fact or piece of information for the first time.

Cross out.
To draw a line through something that you have written, usually because it is wrong.

Look after.
To take care of someone or something by doing what is needed to keep them well or in good condition.

Put away.
To put something in the place where it is usually kept.

Put on.
To put a piece of clothing onto your body.

Final evaluation and performance criteria
Final evaluation and performance criteria

This unit will be evaluated according to the activities that will be developed such as: reading and writing activities by doing some tasks with a certain comprehension issue as well as listening and speaking activities. Students will develop their oral abilities through communicate ideas about personal everyday life.

Summary of the didactic unit
Summary of the didactic unit

Students will work on topics from their everyday life. The vocabulary in this unit is presented in a meaningful context, using interesting reading texts, and it is practiced and extended throughout the unit. The whole unit aims at providing the students with opportunities to practice the language and expand knowledge. The grammar structure is presented in a way that it is easy to understand and guides students to successfully achieve the objectives of the unit. There are also reading texts related to the main grammar focus of the unit. Students are invited to explore and get familiar with the new vocabulary through a variety of activities.
