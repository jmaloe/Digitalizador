Learning activity 1

The context of this activity is a light-hearted Sunday Times article where a journalist went to three big cities, Rome, Paris and New York to find out which was the friendliest towards tourist.

Read the article Big cities from Oxeden, Clive & Latham-Koenig, Christina; and answer exercises 1a and 1b as self-study tasks.

Then, study the information in the Superlatives resource. It will help you understand form and meaning of superlatives.

To complete the activity write a short text (250 - 300 words) in which you describe one of the following:

  • The most polluted city you’ve ever been to.
  • The most impatient person you’ve ever met.
  • The most interesting book you’ve ever read.
  • The most beautiful place you’ve ever seen.

This activity will help you to practice superlatives as you describe your hometown.

Check grammar and spelling before you send your text. Do your activity in an online text.
