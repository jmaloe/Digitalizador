Introduction
Introduction

Welcome to English I !

English plays a key role for those whose goal is to communicate with ease and fluency, to teach English at any level in schools or acquire expertise in the language. This course has been specially designed to develop and improve English as foreign language through materials that can keep students’ enthusiasm and build student’s confidence.

The academic unit English I contains five units. Each unit focuses on different aspects of English grammar, vocabulary, reading, writing and listening; with a little focus on speaking production. They also include revisions of elementary structures, clear and memorable presentations of new structures, regular and motivating practice activities, and reference material. Students have also the opportunity to push themselves to use new language that they are learning and to know how much they are learning.

The first unit deals with the form and use of Present Simple and Present Continuous. It also includes the vocabulary to give personal information, express likes and dislikes, and describe moments and pictures. The second unit revises Past tenses and forms: Simple Past, Present Perfect and Used to. It also provides students with a range of lexical items necessary to talk about past actions and events. Unit three focuses on Present Perfect tense and real life stories and situations that invite the student to share their experiences and achievements. The fourth unit covers the usage of Comparatives and Superlatives, and Phrasal Verbs; it encourages students to learn high-frequency words and phrases. Unit five contains revision and practice of Future forms and models of writing.

Ways of working
Ways of working

This academic unit will be evaluated with the learning evidences as follows:

Online text and word documents

It is a short, clear and understandable text in which students present the result of the activity required. It must be of the length required by the instructions given in the activity. The text must be in an academic writing style with appropriate grammar, vocabulary and punctuation. An online text can take the form of a critical reflective comment which provides with answers, ideas and personal perspectives to specific questions. It can also consist of the elaboration of answers out from the text or audio material. The type of online text will be specified in the corresponding thematic unit.

Forums

It is a space for collaborative work where the student shares information with members of his/her class. It is expected to be a tool for the enrichment and exchange of well-documented participations about the topic being addressed. The information provided by the student in forum participations must reflect beliefs, ideas and points of view. Responses to peers’ contributions are required. At least three participations and three responses to peer’s contributions in forums are mandatory and will be considered for evaluation. Brief and vague participations, as well as repeating or paraphrasing other’s contributions will not be taken into account for evaluation.

Final exam

The student should demonstrate knowledge and understanding of grammar, vocabulary and other features of the language revised in the course by choosing one correct answer. The final exam will be administered at the end of the whole didactic units.

Resit exam
Resit exam

If the student does not have all the necessary elements to pass this academic unit, he/she will have the opportunity to apply for a resist exam at the stipulated date.
