Learning activity 1

Young people tend to make more plans for the future. Two 20 years old people think the following about their lives:

  • “I’m just a year away from graduating. I already work part-time, and hope to get a good full-time job in the company once I graduate. But I have plans for bigger things an MBA as soon as possible, and I’ll be managing a department in the company when I’m 30”.

  • “Although I’m studying, I don’t expect to graduate. My fiancé already has a good job, and we’re getting married next month. I’ll be taking care of baby by the end of next year, I hope. My real ambition is to be good wife and mother”.

What will they be doing in some years from now?

In order to answer this question, revise the Future Continuous grammar point. It will help you to understand the form and use of this tense, as well as understand the differences between Simple Future and Future Continuous. This language referent will help you to remember rules concerning future tenses.

Open the file What will you be doing? from Brewster, Simon; Davies, Paul & Roger’s Mickey; and complete the chart with your own information. Write sentences, and check capitalization, grammar and spelling before submitting your document.

This activity will provide you with practice on the use of Future Continuous.
