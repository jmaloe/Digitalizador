Learning activity 2

For most people, learning a language will be useful at the time of looking for a job and getting better job opportunities in life.

This activity will give you practice writing Curriculum Vitae and a formal “covering” letter; the letter you include with your CV when you send it to a company of organization in response to a job advertisement. The layout and style apply both to letters and emails.

First, open the file Formal letters and CV from Oxeden, Clive, & Latham-Koenig Christina and look at the job advertisement. Then, choose a job from the box. As a self-study tasks, work on exercises b, c and d in the text. This will help you to become familiar with the layout and style of both CV and covering letters.

Next, revise the file Work, which contains useful language and vocabulary regarding this topic. With a dictionary, complete the exercises as self-study tasks.

Use the information in the files labeled Work and Formal letters and CV as guidelines to write your own curriculum and a covering letter to apply for the job you chose.

Send your CV and letter in a word file. Check your document for mistakes before you submit it.

This file must be saved with a name alluding to the subject. To send it, click on “Agregar entrega”. Then, drag and drop the file to start loading. Once complete it, click on  “Guardar cambios”.
