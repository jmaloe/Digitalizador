Learning activity 1

One of the challenges is getting ready for life. Many of us think of learning a language as way to do so. We invest a lot of time to be skillful in speaking, reading, writing and listening to a foreign language because we truly believe it will be a “plus” in our lives. But, how much of a language can you learn in a month?.

Open the file How much you can learn in a month? from Oxeden, Clive, & Latham-Koenig Christina. The main context of this text was inspired by an article in the Sunday Times where an experiment was done to see how well someone could learn a foreign language in just a month. When the month was up, the person travelled to the country itself and carried out a series of tasks to see how much he or she had learnt.

As self-study tasks work on exercises 1a, 1b and 1c, from page 56. Then, revise Modals and check your answers. That language referent will provide you with information concerning the form, use and meaning of modal verbs to express obligation.

Next, in page 57 from the same author, look at the vocabulary section on this file and work on exercises 5a and 5b, which contain adjective modifiers. Give yourself some time to complete them as self-study tasks.

To finish this activity, listen to the audio material “Anna’s test” and answer exercises 3c and 3d.

Send your answers to these exercises in an online-text, and include the nine sentences you completed in exercise 2c of the same file.

This activity aims at providing you with practice on Modal verbs to express obligation and listening for specific information.
