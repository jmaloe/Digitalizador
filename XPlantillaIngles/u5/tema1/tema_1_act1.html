Learning activity 1

The content of this activity is reading a text based on interviews with people arriving in the UK at Heathrow airport in London. Visitors are questioned about plans and arrangements and each has a story to tell.

Open the file Where are you going? from Oxeden, Clive, & Latham-Koenig Christina; and work on the reading exercises 1a, 1b and 1c. These are self-study tasks that will provide preparation for the grammar part of the activity.

As you finished with reading exercises, look at the interview with Maki and Koji and identify six present continuous sentences. Do they refer to the present or to the future? In order to answer this question, revise the file Going to.

It will help you to understand the form and use of going to, as well as understanding the differences between going to and present continuous for future arrangements.

Finish this activity by writing and informal letter. To do it, revise An informal letter. Read the instructions and content in this file and follow the guideline to write and answer to Mrs. Sanders. Check your letter for mistakes. Submit your work in a Word file.

This file must be saved with a name alluding to the subject. To send it, click on “Agregar entrega”. Then, drag and drop the file to start loading. Once complete it, click on “Guardar cambios”.
