Learning activity 1

In 1963, Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. (1929-1968) declared in a speech: “I have a dream today”! He believed it was possible for people of all races and religions in his country to live together in equality and harmony. Thanks to his desires and wishes, the Civil Rights Act was passed, and the Voting Act in 1965, which allowed all adult Americans to vote and end up with a century of racial discrimination.

Revise the Wish file. It will help you to understand the form and use of this grammar point. This language referent will help you to remember rules concerning future tenses.

Open the file My wishes and complete the chart with your own information. Write sentences, and check capitalization, grammar and spelling before submitting your document.

This activity will provide you with practice on the use of wish to express desires.

This file must be saved with a name alluding to the subject. To send it, click on “Agregar entrega”. Then, drag and drop the file to start loading. Once complete it, click on  “Guardar cambios”.
