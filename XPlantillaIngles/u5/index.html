Introduction
Introduction

This unit is focused on future tenses, wish and modals. It aims at providing you with language exposure and language practice that will help you to communicate future plans, arrangements, desires and obligation. The materials included in this unit will make reference to Idiomatic future, Present Continuous, Future Continuous, Wish and Modals.

The activities you will be required to carry out will also be diverse. They go from simple self-study exercises to reading, writing, and listening practice. Special attention will also be drawn to grammatical tenses and forms. In addition, vocabulary will be dealt with as you move though the reading texts and audio materials.

The purpose of the whole unit is to provide students with enough opportunities to activate previous knowledge of English, to revise and learn grammar aspects of the language that are used to express future plans, arrangements, predictions and obligations. Grammar resources have been designed in such a way as to provide students with a clear presentation, guided and free practice, while production tasks need the mastering of key structures. Students will develop listening skills through realistic tasks which reinforce the mastering of the language.

Didactic unit objective(s)
Didactic unit objective(s)

In this academic unit, the student will recognize and use Idiomatic future, Present Continuous, Future Continuous, Wish and Modals; communicate ideas about his/her personal life; acquire vocabulary related to the topics addressed.

Learning result(s)
Learning result(s)

You are competent when:

  • You read short simple texts and look for specific information
  • You understand phrases and common vocabulary on common topics
  • You communicate personal matters in future tenses
  • You communicate accurately by using appropriate grammar forms
  • You skim the texts for main ideas
  • You deduce ideas from context
  • You use the rhetorical forms and conventions of written discourse
  • You listen for specific information

Glossary
Glossary

Look after.
To take care of someone or something by doing what is needed to keep them well or in good condition.

Look for.
To try to find something or someone, either because you have lost them or because you need them.

Look forward to.
To feel pleased and excited about something that is going to happen.

Cambridge University Press (1997) Cambridge International Dictionary of Phrasal Verbs. United Kingdom.

Summary of the didactic unit
Summary of the didactic unit

Students will work on topics about predictions, future plans, future on-going events and modals. The vocabulary in this unit is presented in a meaningful context, using interesting reading texts, and it is practiced and extended throughout the unit. The whole unit aims at providing the students with opportunities to practice the language and expand knowledge.

The grammar structure is presented in a way that it is easy to understand and guides students to successfully achieve the objectives of the unit. There are also reading texts related to the main grammar focus of the unit. Students are invited to explore and get familiar with the new vocabulary through a variety of activities.
