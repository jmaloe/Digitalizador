Introduction
Introduction

It is what we do every day and how we live our life what describes us. Personal information, likes, dislikes and interest are often topics that interest almost everyone who tries to know more about us. Just as well, the world surrounding us offers good opportunities for interactions with others. These two communicative purposes require structures of Present simple tense and Present continuous tense, vocabulary and expressions.

The purpose of the whole unit is to provide students with enough opportunities to activate previous knowledge of English, to revise and learn grammar aspects of the language that are used to express actions, events, routines that take place at the present time, as well as personal information and opinions. Grammar resources have been designed in such a way as to provide students with a clear presentation, guided and free practice, while production tasks need the mastering of key structures. Students will develop listening skills through realistic tasks which reinforce the mastering of the language.

The vocabulary in this unit is presented in a meaningful context, using interesting reading texts, and it is practiced and extended throughout the unit. The whole unit aims at providing the students with opportunities to practice the language and expand their knowledge of it. There are also reading texts related to the main grammar focus of the unit. Students are invited to explore and get familiar with new vocabulary through a variety of activities too. The activities to be carried out will also be diverse. They go from simple self-study tasks involving reading, writing, and listening practice, to tasks in which you demonstrate your understanding of materials and learning.

Didactic unit objective(s)
Didactic unit objective(s)

In this didactic unit, the student will talk about personal information, likes, dislikes and interests by using Simple present tense and appropriate vocabulary; to use Present Continuous and prepositions of time and place to describe events happening at the time of speaking; to distinguish between action and non-action verbs, and to write short texts with discursive composition using connectors.

Learning result(s)
Learning result(s)

You are competent when:

  • You communicate accurately by using appropriate grammar forms.
  • You use the rhetorical forms and conventions of written discourse.
  • You develop and use a battery of listening strategies for overall comprehension.
  • You listen to specific information and identify specific information in spoken discourse.
  • You guess meaning of unknown words from context.

Glossary
Glossary

Cut out
To reduce the amount or number of something.

Eat out
To eat a meal in a restaurant, not at home.

Get on
1. To go onto a bus, train, aircraft, or boat.
2. Mainly British & Australian if two or more people get on, they like each other and are friendly to each other.

Get together
If two or more people get together, or if someone gets together, they meet in order to do something of spend time together.

Heat up
To make food hot so it can be eaten.

Summary of the didactic unit
Summary of the didactic unit

The topics in this unit will provide a context for students to learn about two tenses: Present simple and Present Continuous. Special attention will also be drawn to grammatical tenses and forms as students move through the texts and materials. Useful vocabulary to express personal information, likes and dislikes is also included in the contexts Listening practice will give opportunities for students to be exposed to features of pronunciation of the English language. Writing will be practiced along in each of the activities with a focus on specific rhetoric forms.
