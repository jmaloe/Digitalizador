Learning activity 1

In this activity the present simple in all forms is revised in detail through a British magazine article.

In the file Who knows you better? from Oxeden, Clive, & Latham-Koenig Christina, a family member and a friend both try to choose a suitable partner for a single man who is looking for a girlfriend. Family words and physical description adjective are revised; adjectives of personality are also introduced.

Open the file “Who knows you better?” and look at Part 1 Vocabulary; then, do the puzzle. Use the dictionary if necessary. After that, explore the file Vocabulary Bank: Adjectives and complete the exercises.

Move through the text and read the introduction to the article. After reading it, go to Reading Part C and mark the sentences True or False. Carry out all of the activities as self-study tasks. These activities are necessary as language practice, but they will not be sent.

To complete this activity, revise the Present Simple resource. It will help you to understand the form and use of this tense. This language referent will help you to remember rules concerning to present tense as well as adverbs and expressions of frequency.

Go back to the magazine article and answer exercise 3 Grammar as a self-study task.

Finally, look at the Listening part and read the instructions. You have to listen to Meeting Claire and Meeting Nina audio. Answer Listening exercise a, b and C. Submit your answers in online-text.

This activity will provide you with exposure and practice on the use of simple present and descriptive adjectives.
